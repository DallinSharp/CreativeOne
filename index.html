What is Magic: the Gathering?

Magic: the Gathering is a card game in which two players (typically) take turns drawing and playing cards with different effects and abilities in order to lower their opponent's life total to 0 (or sometimes win in another way).

The two major ways to play Magic: the Gathering are 1-Constructed, where you choose before playing which cards you want to play with, assemble a deck, and bring it to an event and 2-Limited, where you construct a deck on the fly with a limited and semi-randomized assortment of cards to choose from.

Limited has a couple different ways to play, but we're here to give you tips about drafting where normally 8 players start with 3 unopened booster packs containing 15 cards each. To start off, each player opens their first booster pack and selects a single card, then passes that pack of cards to the player to their left. Continue picking one card at a time from the packs that are passed to you until there are no cards left in the first pack. After this, the process is repeated for each of the remaining two packs, except that players pass to the right instead of the left while drafting from the second pack.

Every few months, a new pool of cards is released as a set and standard drafting events use booster packs that contain cards from the new set. Recently, the new set Theros Beyond Death was released, so there are a lot of new cards and choices to make while drafting.
